## Confirmatory Due Diligence Detailed Procedures

### Financial Analysis (Quality of Earnings) - Detailed Process

**Purpose:** Verify the accuracy and sustainability of reported earnings.

**Timeline:** 30-45 days from LOI signing

**Team:**
- Lead: CFO or Financial Analyst
- Support: Bookkeeper, CPA (if needed)

**1. Revenue Verification**

**Process:**
1. **Bank Statement Reconciliation:**
   - Obtain all bank statements for last 3 years
   - Reconcile deposits to revenue per P&L
   - Identify any discrepancies
   - Investigate large deposits that aren't revenue

2. **Tax Return Reconciliation:**
   - Compare P&L revenue to tax return revenue
   - Identify differences and explanations
   - Verify all revenue is reported on tax returns

3. **Revenue Recognition Analysis:**
   - Understand revenue recognition policies
   - Verify revenue is recognized when earned
   - Check for deferred revenue or unearned revenue

4. **Revenue Trend Analysis:**
   - Monthly revenue trends over 3 years
   - Seasonal patterns
   - Growth/decline trends
   - Identify one-time revenue events

**Deliverable:** Revenue Reconciliation Report

**2. Add-Back Validation**

**Purpose:** Verify all add-backs claimed by seller are legitimate.

**Common Add-Backs:**
- Owner salary (above market rate)
- Personal expenses (cars, travel, meals)
- Family members on payroll (not working)
- One-time expenses (legal, consulting, repairs)
- Depreciation (non-cash)
- Interest expense (will be replaced)

**Validation Process:**
1. **Owner Salary:**
   - Research market rate for similar role
   - Verify owner's actual time commitment
   - Calculate appropriate replacement cost
   - Adjust add-back if owner salary is below market

2. **Personal Expenses:**
   - Review all expenses for personal items
   - Verify with receipts and documentation
   - Calculate total personal expenses
   - Ensure these are truly personal (not business)

3. **Family Members:**
   - Verify family members on payroll
   - Assess if they're actually working
   - Calculate appropriate replacement cost
   - Adjust add-back if family members are working

4. **One-Time Expenses:**
   - Identify one-time vs. recurring expenses
   - Verify expenses are truly one-time
   - Calculate total one-time expenses
   - Ensure expenses won't recur

**Deliverable:** Add-Back Validation Report with adjustments

**3. Working Capital Analysis**

**Purpose:** Determine appropriate working capital target (PEG).

**Process:**
1. **Calculate Historical Working Capital:**
   - Current Assets - Current Liabilities
   - Calculate for each month over last 12 months
   - Identify average and range

2. **Normalize Working Capital:**
   - Adjust for seasonality
   - Adjust for one-time items
   - Calculate normalized working capital

3. **Set Working Capital Target (PEG):**
   - Typically 30-60 days of revenue
   - Adjust based on industry and business model
   - Ensure adequate cash for operations

4. **Working Capital Adjustment:**
   - Calculate difference between actual and target
   - Adjust purchase price if needed
   - Structure as working capital true-up

**Deliverable:** Working Capital Analysis Report

**4. Customer Churn Analysis**

**Purpose:** Understand customer retention and identify risks.

**Process:**
1. **Customer List Analysis:**
   - Obtain customer list for last 3 years
   - Identify active vs. inactive customers
   - Calculate retention rates by year
   - Identify customers at risk

2. **Revenue Concentration:**
   - Identify top 10 customers
   - Calculate revenue concentration
   - Assess risk of customer loss
   - Verify customer contracts

3. **Customer Lifetime Value:**
   - Calculate average customer lifetime
   - Calculate average customer value
   - Identify high-value customers
   - Assess retention strategies

**Deliverable:** Customer Analysis Report

**5. Accounts Receivable Analysis**

**Purpose:** Verify AR is collectible and properly aged.

**Process:**
1. **AR Aging Report:**
   - Obtain detailed AR aging report
   - Analyze by age buckets (0-30, 31-60, 61-90, 90+)
   - Identify past-due accounts
   - Calculate collection rates by age

2. **Collectibility Assessment:**
   - Review collection history
   - Identify bad debt patterns
   - Calculate expected collection rate
   - Adjust AR value if needed

3. **Customer Credit Analysis:**
   - Review credit policies
   - Assess customer creditworthiness
   - Identify high-risk customers
   - Verify credit limits

**Deliverable:** AR Analysis Report with collectibility assessment

### Legal Review - Detailed Process

**Purpose:** Identify legal risks and ensure clean transfer.

**Timeline:** 30-45 days from LOI signing

**Team:**
- Lead: M&A Attorney
- Support: Corporate Attorney, Employment Attorney (if needed)

**1. Entity Status Verification**

**Process:**
1. **Good Standing:**
   - Verify entity is in good standing with state
   - Check for any pending dissolution
   - Verify annual reports are filed
   - Check for any penalties or fees

2. **UCC Search:**
   - Conduct UCC search for liens
   - Identify all secured creditors
   - Verify lien priority
   - Ensure liens can be released at closing

3. **Judgment Search:**
   - Search for judgments against entity
   - Identify any outstanding judgments
   - Verify judgment status
   - Ensure judgments are resolved before closing

**Deliverable:** Entity Status Report

**2. Contract Review**

**Purpose:** Identify contracts that may be affected by change of control.

**Key Contracts to Review:**
- Customer contracts (service agreements)
- Vendor contracts (supply agreements)
- Lease agreements (real estate, equipment)
- Loan agreements (debt covenants)
- Insurance policies
- License agreements

**Review Process:**
1. **Change of Control Clauses:**
   - Identify contracts with change of control provisions
   - Assess impact on transaction
   - Determine if consent is required
   - Plan for obtaining consents

2. **Assignment Clauses:**
   - Identify contracts with assignment restrictions
   - Assess if assignment is permitted
   - Determine if consent is required
   - Plan for obtaining consents

3. **Termination Clauses:**
   - Identify contracts with termination rights
   - Assess risk of termination
   - Plan for contract continuity

**Deliverable:** Contract Review Report with risk assessment

**3. Employee Agreement Review**

**Purpose:** Verify employee agreements protect the business.

**Key Agreements:**
- Employment agreements
- Non-compete agreements
- Non-solicit agreements
- Confidentiality agreements
- IP assignment agreements

**Review Process:**
1. **Non-Compete Agreements:**
   - Verify key employees have non-competes
   - Assess enforceability of non-competes
   - Identify gaps in coverage
   - Plan for additional agreements if needed

2. **Non-Solicit Agreements:**
   - Verify key employees have non-solicits
   - Assess enforceability
   - Identify gaps in coverage

3. **IP Assignment:**
   - Verify employees have assigned IP
   - Identify any IP not assigned
   - Plan for IP assignment if needed

**Deliverable:** Employee Agreement Review Report

**4. Litigation History**

**Purpose:** Identify past and pending litigation risks.

**Process:**
1. **Litigation Search:**
   - Search court records for past litigation
   - Identify pending litigation
   - Review litigation outcomes
   - Assess ongoing litigation risks

2. **Threat Assessment:**
   - Identify potential litigation threats
   - Assess likelihood of litigation
   - Estimate potential liability
   - Plan for risk mitigation

**Deliverable:** Litigation History Report

**5. Environmental Assessment**

**Purpose:** Identify environmental risks (if real estate involved).

**Process:**
1. **Phase I ESA:**
   - Conduct Phase I Environmental Site Assessment
   - Identify recognized environmental conditions
   - Assess environmental risks
   - Plan for remediation if needed

2. **Compliance Review:**
   - Review environmental compliance
   - Identify any violations
   - Assess compliance risks
   - Plan for compliance improvements

**Deliverable:** Environmental Assessment Report

**4. Key Employee Interviews**

**Process:**

2. **Interview Process:**

3. **Retention Planning:**

**Deliverable:** Key Employee Assessment Report with retention plan

## Deal Structuring - Detailed Procedures

### Valuation Methods - Comprehensive Guide

**1. Multiple-Based Valuation**

**Process:**
1. **SDE/EBITDA Calculation:**
   - Calculate normalized SDE/EBITDA
   - Adjust for add-backs
   - Adjust for one-time items
   - Calculate final SDE/EBITDA

2. **Multiple Selection:**
   - Research market multiples
   - Adjust for business characteristics
   - Select appropriate multiple
   - Calculate enterprise value

3. **Adjustments:**
   - Add inventory (at cost)
   - Add working capital (if needed)
   - Subtract debt (if assumed)
   - Calculate equity value

**Multiple Ranges:**
- Small businesses (<$500k SDE): 2.0x - 3.0x SDE
- Medium businesses ($500k-$1M EBITDA): 3.0x - 4.0x EBITDA
- Large businesses (>$1M EBITDA): 3.5x - 4.5x EBITDA

**Factors Affecting Multiple:**
- Recurring revenue percentage
- Growth rate
- Market position
- Management depth
- Customer concentration
- Industry trends

### Financing Structure - Detailed Guide

**1. SBA 7(a) Loan**

**Structure:**
- Amount: 70-80% of project cost
- Term: 10 years
- Rate: Prime + 2.25% - 2.75%
- Collateral: Business assets + personal guarantee
- Use: Working capital, equipment, real estate

**Requirements:**
- Good credit (680+)
- Down payment (10-30%)
- Business plan
- Financial projections
- Personal financial statement

**Process:**
1. Pre-approval (before LOI)
2. Application (after LOI)
3. Underwriting (30-60 days)
4. Approval
5. Closing

**2. Seller Financing**

**Structure:**
- Amount: 10-20% of purchase price
- Term: 5-7 years
- Rate: 4-6% (below market)
- Payments: Standby for 2 years, then amortized
- Collateral: Business assets (subordinate to SBA)

**Benefits:**
- Aligns seller with buyer success
- Reduces equity requirement
- Lower interest rate
- Flexible terms

**3. Equity Injection**

**Structure:**
- Amount: 10% of purchase price
- Source: PNR Holdings/Investors
- Use: Down payment, working capital
- Return: Equity ownership in OpCo

### OpCo/PropCo Split - Detailed Process

**Purpose:** Separate operations from real estate for asset protection and tax efficiency.

**Process:**
1. **Real Estate Appraisal:**
   - Appraise real estate separately
   - Determine fair market value
   - Allocate purchase price

2. **Entity Formation:**
   - Form PropCo (Obuke LLC)
   - Form OpCo (if needed)
   - Set up lease structure

3. **Purchase Structure:**
   - OpCo buys business assets
   - PropCo buys real estate
   - OpCo leases from PropCo

4. **Lease Terms:**
   - Term: 10-20 years
   - Type: Triple-net (NNN)
   - Rate: Market rate
   - Escalations: Annual CPI adjustments

**Benefits:**
- Asset protection
- Tax efficiency
- Financing flexibility
- Exit flexibility
