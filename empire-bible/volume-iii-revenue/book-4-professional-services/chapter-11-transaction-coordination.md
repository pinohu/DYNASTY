## Section 11.1: SubTo Certification & Implementation

"SubTo" refers to acquiring property "subject to" the existing mortgage. This creative financing strategy allows investors to acquire properties without qualifying for new loans, but requires specialized knowledge to avoid "due-on-sale" triggers and ensure proper insurance and servicing transfer. Mastering SubTo coordination positions you as an expert in the highest-value transaction type.

**Key Concepts to Master:**

**1. Deed of Trust vs. Warranty Deed**
- **Deed of Trust**: Security instrument, transfers title to trustee
- **Warranty Deed**: Transfers ownership, guarantees title
- **Understanding**: Know the difference and when each is used
- **Application**: Use correct documents in SubTo transactions

**2. Power of Attorney (Limited)**
- **Purpose**: Authorize communication with seller's lender
- **Scope**: Limited to specific transactions
- **Requirements**: Must be properly executed and notarized
- **Application**: Essential for SubTo transactions

**3. Authorization to Release Information**
- **Purpose**: First document needed, authorizes lender communication
- **Timing**: Must be signed before contacting lender
- **Requirements**: Proper execution and notarization
- **Application**: Critical first step in SubTo process

**4. Due-on-Sale Clause**
- **Understanding**: Know what it is and how it works
- **Mitigation**: Understand mitigation strategies (land trusts, insurance)
- **Communication**: Explain to investors clearly
- **Risk Management**: Help investors manage risk

**5. Land Trust Structures**
- **Purpose**: Asset protection and privacy
- **Structure**: How land trusts work in SubTo
- **Implementation**: How to set up land trusts
- **Benefits**: Benefits for investors

**6. Insurance Structuring**
- **Challenge**: Trickiest part of SubTo transactions
- **Requirements**: New policy listing Trust as insured, Lender as mortgagee
- **Process**: How to structure insurance correctly
- **Risk**: Avoid loan being called due to insurance issues
