# Cold Call Introduction Script

## Purpose

This script provides a proven framework for cold calling prospects to introduce your services and schedule discovery calls. Use this script as a starting point and customize based on your service offerings.

## Instructions

1. **Customize**: Adapt script to your specific services and value propositions
2. **Practice**: Practice the script until it feels natural
3. **Personalize**: Research prospects before calling to personalize opening
4. **Listen**: Focus on listening and responding to prospect's needs
5. **Follow Up**: Always follow up as promised

## Script

### Opening (First 30 Seconds)

**Goal**: Get attention and permission to continue

**Script**:
> "Hi [PROSPECT NAME], this is [YOUR NAME] from [COMPANY NAME]. I know you weren't expecting my call - do you have a quick minute, or is now a bad time?"

**If "Bad Time"**:
> "No problem at all. When would be a better time to call you back - later today or tomorrow?"

**If "Yes, I have a minute"**:
> "Great! I'm calling because we help [TARGET MARKET] [KEY BENEFIT]. I noticed [SPECIFIC REASON FOR CALLING - e.g., your company just expanded, you're in the real estate industry, etc.]. Is that something you're dealing with?"

### Value Proposition (Next 30 Seconds)

**Goal**: Establish credibility and create interest

**Script**:
> "We specialize in [SPECIFIC SERVICE] for companies like yours. Specifically, we help [TARGET MARKET] [PRIMARY BENEFIT] by [HOW YOU DO IT]. For example, we recently helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] [SPECIFIC RESULT]."

**Key Elements**:
- Be specific about who you help
- Mention a concrete benefit
- Provide a brief example or social proof
- Keep it concise

### Discovery Question (Next 30 Seconds)

**Goal**: Understand prospect's situation and needs

**Script**:
> "I'm curious - are you currently [DOING SOMETHING RELATED TO YOUR SERVICE], or is that something you're looking to [IMPROVE/IMPLEMENT]?"

**Alternative Questions**:
- "What's your biggest challenge with [RELEVANT TOPIC] right now?"
- "How are you currently handling [RELEVANT PROCESS]?"
- "What would [DESIRED OUTCOME] mean for your business?"

**Listen Carefully**: The answer will guide your next steps.

### Handle Objections (If Needed)

**Common Objections and Responses**:

**"We're not interested"**:
> "I understand - I'm not trying to sell you anything today. I'm just trying to see if there's a fit. What specifically makes you say you're not interested?"

**"We already have a provider"**:
> "That's great that you have someone handling that. What made you choose them? Are you completely satisfied, or are there areas where you'd like to see improvement?"

**"We don't have budget"**:
> "I understand budget is always a consideration. What would need to happen for this to become a priority? What's the cost of not addressing [THEIR CHALLENGE]?"

**"Send me information"**:
> "I'd be happy to send you some information. But first, let me make sure I send you something relevant. What specific challenges are you facing with [TOPIC]?"

### Qualify (Next 30 Seconds)

**Goal**: Determine if prospect is a good fit

**Key Questions**:
- "How many [RELEVANT METRIC] do you typically [DO] per month?"
- "Who's involved in making decisions about [RELEVANT TOPIC]?"
- "What's your timeline for [RELEVANT GOAL]?"
- "What's your budget range for [RELEVANT SOLUTION]?"

### Close for Next Step (Final 30 Seconds)

**Goal**: Schedule discovery call or meeting

**Script**:
> "Based on what you've told me, I think there might be a fit here. Would it make sense to schedule a quick 15-minute call next week to explore this further? I can show you how we've helped companies like yours [ACHIEVE SPECIFIC RESULT]."

**If "Yes"**:
> "Great! What works better for you - [DAY] morning or [DAY] afternoon? And what's the best email to send you a calendar invite?"

**If "Maybe" or "Not Sure"**:
> "I understand you want to think about it. What questions can I answer that would help you decide if this is worth exploring?"

**If "No"**:
> "I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask what specifically doesn't fit? That way I can make sure I'm reaching out to the right people."

### Closing (If No Meeting)

**Goal**: Leave door open for future contact

**Script**:
> "I understand. Thanks for your time today. Would it be okay if I follow up in [TIME PERIOD] to see if anything has changed? And can I send you some information via email so you have it if you need it?"

## Full Script Example

### Example: Notary Services

**Opening**:
> "Hi Sarah, this is Jennifer from Notroom Services. I know you weren't expecting my call - do you have a quick minute, or is now a bad time?"

**Value Proposition**:
> "Great! I'm calling because we help real estate professionals streamline their notary needs. I noticed your company handles a lot of real estate transactions. We specialize in providing fast, reliable notary and RON services for title companies and real estate agents. For example, we recently helped ABC Title Company reduce their notary wait times by 60%."

**Discovery**:
> "I'm curious - how are you currently handling notary services for your transactions? Are you using in-house notaries or outsourcing?"

**Qualify**:
> "How many transactions do you typically close per month? And who's involved in selecting notary service providers?"

**Close**:
> "Based on what you've told me, I think there might be a fit here. Would it make sense to schedule a quick 15-minute call next week to explore how we could help streamline your notary process? I can show you how we've helped other title companies reduce costs and improve efficiency."

## Best Practices

### Before the Call
- [ ] Research the prospect and their company
- [ ] Identify specific reason for calling
- [ ] Prepare personalized opening
- [ ] Have calendar ready to schedule
- [ ] Prepare objection responses

### During the Call
- [ ] Smile (it comes through in your voice)
- [ ] Stand up (increases energy)
- [ ] Use prospect's name naturally
- [ ] Listen more than you talk
- [ ] Take notes
- [ ] Be conversational, not scripted

### After the Call
- [ ] Send calendar invite immediately (if scheduled)
- [ ] Send follow-up email with value proposition
- [ ] Add to CRM with notes
- [ ] Set reminder for follow-up (if needed)
- [ ] Update script based on what worked/didn't work

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Talking Too Much**: Let the prospect talk - you learn more
2. **Being Too Salesy**: Focus on helping, not selling
3. **Not Listening**: Listen to what they say and respond accordingly
4. **Giving Up Too Early**: Many sales require multiple touches
5. **Not Following Up**: Always follow up as promised

## Metrics to Track

- Calls made
- Connections made (answered calls)
- Discovery calls scheduled
- Discovery calls completed
- Qualified opportunities created
- Conversion rate (calls to opportunities)
