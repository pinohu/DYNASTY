# SOP: Invoicing and Accounts Receivable

## Purpose

This SOP defines the process for generating invoices, sending them to clients, and collecting payments. It aims to maximize cash flow and minimize overdue accounts.

## Scope

Applies to all billing activities for [COMPANY NAME].

## Responsibilities

-   **Billing Specialist**: Generating and sending invoices.
-   **Account Manager**: Resolving billing disputes.
-   **Finance Manager**: Reviewing aging reports.

## Process Steps

### 1. Invoice Generation
-   [ ] Review billable hours/milestones for the period.
-   [ ] Verify expense reimbursements.
-   [ ] Generate invoice in accounting software (e.g., QuickBooks).
-   [ ] Verify invoice accuracy (amounts, dates, client details).

### 2. Sending Invoices
-   [ ] Send invoice via email to client billing contact.
-   [ ] Attach detailed time logs or receipts if required.
-   [ ] Confirm receipt (if automated system allows).
-   **Timing**: Invoices sent by the [DAY] of the month.

### 3. Payment Monitoring
-   [ ] Monitor bank feed for incoming payments.
-   [ ] Apply payments to open invoices in accounting software.

### 4. Collections (Overdue Accounts)
-   **3 Days Past Due**: Automated reminder email.
-   **15 Days Past Due**: Personal email from Billing Specialist.
-   **30 Days Past Due**: Phone call from Account Manager.
-   **60 Days Past Due**: Service suspension warning.
-   **90 Days Past Due**: Legal action/Collections agency.

## Quality Standards

-   **Accuracy**: 100% invoice accuracy.
-   **Timeliness**: Invoices sent within 3 days of billing cycle end.
-   **DSO (Days Sales Outstanding)**: Target < 45 days.
