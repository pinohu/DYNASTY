# Crummey Trust Template

## Purpose

This template provides a Crummey Trust Agreement for minor children, allowing annual exclusion gifting ($18,000 per child per contributor) while maintaining control over assets until children reach specified ages. These trusts merge into the Dynasty Trust when children reach age 25.

**IMPORTANT**: This template must be reviewed and customized by qualified estate planning counsel before execution.

## Instructions

1. **Identify Child**: Replace [CHILD NAME] with actual child's name
2. **Set Distribution Schedule**: Customize age-based distribution schedule
3. **Select Trustee**: Choose appropriate trustee (cannot be grantor)
4. **Structure Crummey Powers**: Ensure withdrawal rights are properly structured
5. **Define Investment Strategy**: Specify investment guidelines
6. **Review Tax Implications**: Have tax advisor review for gift tax exclusion
7. **Execute**: Execute trust agreement
8. **Annual Gifting**: Make annual contributions and send Crummey notices

## Template

```markdown
# CRUMMEY TRUST FOR [CHILD NAME]

## TRUST IDENTIFICATION

**Trust Name**: [CHILD NAME] Crummey Trust  
**Date of Execution**: [DATE]  
**Grantor**: [GRANTOR NAME]  
**Trustee**: [TRUSTEE NAME]  
**Beneficiary**: [CHILD NAME] (born [DATE])  
**EIN**: [EIN NUMBER - OBTAIN AFTER EXECUTION]

---

## ARTICLE I: TRUST CREATION

### Section 1.1: Trust Purpose

This Trust is created to receive annual exclusion gifts for the benefit of [CHILD NAME], with the following objectives:

(a) Qualify gifts for the annual gift tax exclusion ($18,000 per year per contributor, adjusted for inflation);

(b) Provide asset protection for the Beneficiary;

(c) Control distributions until the Beneficiary reaches specified ages;

(d) Invest assets for the Beneficiary's benefit;

(e) Merge into the Dynasty Trust when the Beneficiary reaches age 25.

### Section 1.2: Irrevocability

This Trust is irrevocable. The Grantor retains no powers that would cause the Trust to be included in the Grantor's estate for estate tax purposes.

### Section 1.3: Governing Law

This Trust shall be governed by the laws of [STATE], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

---

## ARTICLE II: CONTRIBUTIONS

### Section 2.1: Annual Contributions

The Grantor or others may make contributions to this Trust up to the annual gift tax exclusion amount ($18,000 per year per contributor, adjusted for inflation).

**2024 Annual Exclusion**: $18,000 per contributor per beneficiary

**Multiple Contributors**: If both parents contribute, total annual contributions may be up to $36,000 ($18,000 Ã— 2).

### Section 2.2: Crummey Withdrawal Rights

Upon receiving a contribution, the Beneficiary shall have the right to withdraw the contribution within 30 days of receiving written notice.

**Purpose**: Crummey withdrawal rights allow contributions to qualify for the annual gift tax exclusion.

### Section 2.3: Crummey Notice Requirements

The Trustee shall provide written notice to the Beneficiary (or legal guardian if Beneficiary is a minor) within 7 days of receiving a contribution, specifying:

(a) The amount of the contribution;

(b) The Beneficiary's withdrawal right;

(c) The deadline for exercising the withdrawal right (30 days from notice date);

(d) Instructions for exercising the withdrawal right (written notice to Trustee);

(e) The consequences of not exercising the withdrawal right (lapse and addition to Trust principal).

### Section 2.4: Withdrawal Exercise

The Beneficiary (or legal guardian if minor) may exercise the withdrawal right by:

(a) Providing written notice to the Trustee within 30 days;

(b) Specifying the amount to be withdrawn (up to the Beneficiary's withdrawal right);

(c) Providing instructions for distribution (check, wire transfer, etc.).

### Section 2.5: Lapse of Withdrawal Rights

If the Beneficiary does not exercise the withdrawal right within 30 days, the withdrawal right shall lapse, and the contribution shall become part of the Trust principal.

### Section 2.6: Lapse Limitations

To avoid gift tax consequences, withdrawal rights that lapse in excess of the greater of $5,000 or 5% of the Trust value may be subject to gift tax. The Trustee shall monitor this and may structure contributions accordingly.

---

## ARTICLE III: DISTRIBUTIONS

### Section 3.1: Distributions During Minority

While the Beneficiary is under age 25, the Trustee may make distributions for:

(a) Health, education, maintenance, and support (HEMS);

(b) Other purposes as the Trustee deems appropriate in his or her sole discretion.

**HEMS Standard**: For purposes of this Trust:

- **Health**: Medical, dental, mental health expenses, health insurance premiums, wellness expenses
- **Education**: Tuition, fees, educational materials, study abroad programs, professional development
- **Maintenance**: Reasonable housing, transportation, food, clothing, utilities
- **Support**: Expenses necessary to maintain the Beneficiary's accustomed standard of living

### Section 3.2: Mandatory Distributions

The Trustee shall distribute:

- **Age 25**: 25% of the Trust principal shall be distributed outright to the Beneficiary
- **Age 30**: 50% of the remaining principal shall be distributed outright to the Beneficiary
- **Age 35**: 100% of the remaining principal shall be distributed outright to the Beneficiary

**Prior to Age 25**: All distributions shall be for HEMS purposes only.

### Section 3.3: Early Termination

The Trust may terminate earlier if:

(a) All Trust assets are distributed;

(b) The Beneficiary dies (assets pass to contingent beneficiaries as set forth in Article VI);

(c) The Trustee determines termination is in the Beneficiary's best interest (with court approval if required).

---

## ARTICLE IV: INVESTMENT PROVISIONS

### Section 4.1: Investment Authority

The Trustee may invest Trust assets in any type of property, including:

(a) Stocks, bonds, and mutual funds;

(b) Real estate;

(c) Business interests (including family businesses);

(d) Cash and cash equivalents;

(e) Alternative investments (with appropriate due diligence);

(f) Any other investment permitted by law and this Agreement.

### Section 4.2: Investment Policy

Investments shall be made in accordance with the Investment Policy Statement (if any), considering:

(a) The Beneficiary's age and time horizon;

(b) Risk tolerance appropriate for the Beneficiary;

(c) Diversification requirements;

(d) Tax efficiency;

(e) Growth potential;

(f) Preservation of capital.

### Section 4.3: Investment in Family Businesses

The Trustee may invest Trust assets in family businesses, provided:

(a) The investment is prudent and in the Beneficiary's best interests;

(b) The investment is properly documented;

(c) The investment complies with applicable laws and regulations.

### Section 4.4: Prohibited Investments

Notwithstanding Section 4.1, the Trust Estate shall not be invested in:

(a) Collectibles (as defined in Section 408(m) of the Internal Revenue Code);

(b) Life insurance policies on the Beneficiary's life;

(c) Any investment that would cause adverse tax consequences;

(d) Investments that violate applicable laws or regulations.

---

## ARTICLE V: MERGER PROVISION

### Section 5.1: Merger into Dynasty Trust

When the Beneficiary reaches age 25, this Trust shall merge into the [DYNASTY TRUST NAME], and the Beneficiary's interest shall be held in a sub-trust of the Dynasty Trust.

**Merger Date**: The merger shall occur on the Beneficiary's 25th birthday, or as soon as practicable thereafter.

### Section 5.2: Merger Process

The merger shall be accomplished by:

(a) Transferring all Trust assets to the Dynasty Trust;

(b) Creating a sub-trust for the Beneficiary under the Dynasty Trust (if not already existing);

(c) Distributing 25% of the Trust principal to the Beneficiary (as set forth in Section 3.2);

(d) Transferring the remaining 75% to the Dynasty Trust sub-trust;

(e) Terminating this Trust.

### Section 5.3: Merger Documentation

The Trustee shall:

(a) Prepare merger documentation;

(b) Obtain any required approvals;

(c) File any required tax returns;

(d) Complete the merger in accordance with applicable laws.

---

## ARTICLE VI: TRUSTEE

### Section 6.1: Initial Trustee

The initial Trustee shall be [TRUSTEE NAME], who shall serve until resignation, removal, or death.

**Trustee Qualifications**: The Trustee must be an independent party who is not the Grantor or a Beneficiary.

### Section 6.2: Successor Trustee

Upon resignation, removal, or death of the Trustee, the successor Trustee shall be [SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE NAME], or if such person is unable or unwilling to serve, [ALTERNATE SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE NAME].

### Section 6.3: Trustee Powers

The Trustee shall have all powers necessary to administer the Trust, including:

(a) Investment powers (as set forth in Article IV);

(b) Distribution powers (as set forth in Article III);

(c) Administrative powers (as set forth in Article VII);

(d) Power to hire advisors and agents;

(e) Power to execute all documents necessary to administer the Trust;

(f) Any other powers granted by law or this Agreement.

### Section 6.4: Trustee Compensation

The Trustee shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, which shall be paid from Trust assets.

### Section 6.5: Trustee Liability

The Trustee shall not be liable for any action taken or omitted in good faith, except for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

---

## ARTICLE VII: TRUST ADMINISTRATION

### Section 7.1: Accounting

The Trustee shall:

(a) Maintain accurate books and records of all Trust transactions;

(b) Provide annual accountings to the Beneficiary (or legal guardian if Beneficiary is a minor);

(c) File all required tax returns (Form 1041 if the Trust has taxable income);

(d) Maintain records for a minimum of seven years (or as required by law).

### Section 7.2: Tax Returns

The Trustee shall file Form 1041 (U.S. Income Tax Return for Estates and Trusts) if the Trust has taxable income in any year.

### Section 7.3: Tax Elections

The Trustee, in consultation with tax advisors, may make any tax elections available to the Trust, including:

(a) Fiscal year elections;

(b) Distribution elections;

(c) Other elections as may be beneficial to the Trust and Beneficiary.

### Section 7.4: Crummey Notices

The Trustee shall send Crummey notices annually for all contributions, ensuring compliance with gift tax exclusion requirements.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: CONTINGENT BENEFICIARIES

### Section 8.1: Death of Beneficiary

If the Beneficiary dies before the Trust terminates, the remaining Trust assets shall be distributed to:

**Option A - Other Children**:
Equally among the Grantor's other children, per stirpes.

**Option B - Dynasty Trust**:
To the [DYNASTY TRUST NAME] to be held for the benefit of the Beneficiary's descendants, per stirpes.

**Option C - Specific Contingent Beneficiaries**:
[SPECIFY CONTINGENT BENEFICIARIES]

### Section 8.2: Per Stirpes Distribution

If distribution is per stirpes, the Beneficiary's descendants shall take by right of representation, with each branch of the family receiving an equal share.

---

## ARTICLE IX: ASSET PROTECTION

### Section 9.1: Spendthrift Provisions

No Beneficiary shall have the power to:

(a) Assign, transfer, or encumber his or her interest in the Trust;

(b) Anticipate distributions;

(c) Subject his or her interest to the claims of creditors, except as provided in Section 9.2.

### Section 9.2: Creditor Protection

The Trust Estate shall be protected from the claims of creditors of the Grantor, Beneficiary, and Trustees, except:

(a) Claims for child support or alimony (to the extent required by law);

(b) Claims by the United States or any state for taxes (to the extent required by law);

(c) Claims that existed at the time property was transferred to the Trust (subject to applicable statutes of limitations and fraudulent transfer laws).

---

## ARTICLE X: AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION

### Section 10.1: Amendment

This Trust may be amended by [TRUST PROTECTOR/COURT ORDER], subject to:

(a) The amendment must not cause the Trust to be included in the Grantor's estate;

(b) The amendment must not adversely affect the rights of Beneficiaries without their consent (if required by law);

(c) The amendment must be in writing and properly executed.

### Section 10.2: Termination

This Trust shall terminate:

(a) Upon distribution of all Trust assets;

(b) Upon merger into the Dynasty Trust (as set forth in Article V);

(c) Upon court order for good cause shown;

(d) Upon written direction of the Trust Protector (subject to limitations and court approval if required).

---

## ARTICLE XI: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 11.1: Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 11.2: Headings

Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

### Section 11.3: Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in multiple counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

### Section 11.4: Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding the Trust and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.
```
