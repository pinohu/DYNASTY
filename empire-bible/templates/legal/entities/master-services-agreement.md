# Master Services Agreement Template

## Purpose

This template provides a comprehensive Master Services Agreement for inter-entity service arrangements within the Ohu-PNR Dynasty Empire structure. This agreement governs the provision of management, administrative, and other services from CXI LLC (the management company) to operating entities.

**IMPORTANT**: This template must be reviewed and customized by qualified legal counsel before execution. Pricing and terms must comply with transfer pricing regulations.

## Instructions

1. **Identify Parties**: Replace [MANAGEMENT COMPANY] and [OPERATING COMPANY] with actual entity names
2. **Define Services**: Complete Schedule A with specific services to be provided
3. **Set Pricing**: Establish arm's-length pricing for all services (consult transfer pricing advisor)
4. **Review Transfer Pricing**: Ensure pricing complies with IRS transfer pricing regulations
5. **Customize Terms**: Adjust terms based on specific business needs
6. **Execute**: Have authorized representatives sign the agreement
7. **Document**: Maintain documentation for transfer pricing compliance

## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Provider is willing to provide such services to Recipient on the terms and conditions set forth herein;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE I: DEFINITIONS

### 1.1 Definitions

**"Agreement"** means this Master Services Agreement, including all Schedules attached hereto.

**"Services"** means the services described in Schedule A attached hereto.

**"Service Fees"** means the fees for Services as set forth in Schedule B attached hereto.

**"Term"** means the initial term and any renewal terms as set forth in Article VI.

**"Confidential Information"** means all non-public, proprietary, or confidential information disclosed by one party to the other.

---

## ARTICLE II: SERVICES

### 2.1 Provision of Services

Provider agrees to provide to Recipient the Services described in Schedule A, in accordance with the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

### 2.2 Service Standards

Provider shall provide Services:

(a) In a professional and workmanlike manner;

(b) In accordance with industry standards and best practices;

(c) In compliance with all applicable laws and regulations;

(d) Using qualified personnel and appropriate resources.

### 2.3 Service Modifications

Recipient may request modifications to Services by providing written notice to Provider. Provider shall use reasonable efforts to accommodate such requests, subject to:

(a) Provider's capacity and resources;

(b) Additional fees for modified or additional services;

(c) Written agreement on modified terms.

### 2.4 Subcontractors

Provider may engage subcontractors to perform Services, provided that:

(a) Provider remains responsible for all Services;

(b) Subcontractors are bound by confidentiality obligations;

(c) Provider ensures subcontractor compliance with this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE III: COMPENSATION

### 3.1 Service Fees

Recipient shall pay Provider the Service Fees set forth in Schedule B for Services provided hereunder.

### 3.2 Payment Terms

Service Fees shall be:

(a) Invoiced monthly in advance (or as otherwise specified in Schedule B);

(b) Due within [NUMBER] days of invoice date;

(c) Paid by wire transfer or check to Provider's designated account;

(d) Subject to late fees of [PERCENTAGE]% per month on overdue amounts.

### 3.3 Additional Services

Services not included in Schedule A shall be provided at Provider's then-current rates or as otherwise agreed in writing.

### 3.4 Expenses

Recipient shall reimburse Provider for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses incurred in providing Services, provided such expenses are:

(a) Pre-approved by Recipient (for expenses over $[AMOUNT]);

(b) Documented with receipts;

(c) Invoiced separately with supporting documentation.

### 3.5 Transfer Pricing

The parties acknowledge that Service Fees are established at arm's length and in compliance with transfer pricing regulations under Section 482 of the Internal Revenue Code.

---

## ARTICLE IV: TERM AND TERMINATION

### 4.1 Initial Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial term of [NUMBER] years, unless earlier terminated in accordance with this Agreement.

### 4.2 Renewal

This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive [NUMBER]-year terms unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least [NUMBER] days prior to the end of the then-current term.

### 4.3 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:

(a) The other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure such breach within [NUMBER] days of written notice;

(b) The other party becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy;

(c) The other party ceases to conduct business in the ordinary course.

### 4.4 Termination Without Cause

Either party may terminate this Agreement without cause upon [NUMBER] days' written notice to the other party.

### 4.5 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

(a) Provider shall complete Services in progress (subject to payment);

(b) Recipient shall pay all outstanding Service Fees and expenses;

(c) Each party shall return or destroy Confidential Information;

(d) Provisions that by their nature survive termination shall continue in effect.

---

## ARTICLE V: CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Confidential Information

Each party acknowledges that it may receive Confidential Information from the other party. Each party agrees to:

(a) Hold Confidential Information in strict confidence;

(b) Use Confidential Information solely for purposes of this Agreement;

(c) Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties without prior written consent;

(d) Take reasonable precautions to protect Confidential Information.

### 5.2 Exceptions

The confidentiality obligations shall not apply to information that:

(a) Is publicly available or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;

(b) Was known to the receiving party prior to disclosure;

(c) Is independently developed by the receiving party;

(d) Is required to be disclosed by law or court order (subject to notice and opportunity to contest).

### 5.3 Return of Information

Upon termination, each party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information, except as required for record-keeping or legal compliance.

---

## ARTICLE VI: INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 6.1 Provider IP

All intellectual property owned by Provider prior to this Agreement or developed independently of Services ("Provider IP") shall remain the property of Provider.

### 6.2 Work Product

Intellectual property developed specifically for Recipient in the course of providing Services ("Work Product") shall be owned by Recipient, subject to Provider's right to use general methodologies and know-how.

### 6.3 License

Provider grants Recipient a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use Provider IP solely in connection with Services, for the Term of this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE VII: WARRANTIES AND DISCLAIMERS

### 7.1 Provider Warranties

Provider warrants that:

(a) It has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement;

(b) Services will be performed in a professional manner;

(c) Services will comply with applicable laws and regulations.

### 7.2 Disclaimers

EXCEPT AS EXPRESSLY SET FORTH HEREIN, PROVIDER MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WARRANTIES OF MERCHANTABILITY OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

### 7.3 Recipient Warranties

Recipient warrants that:

(a) It has the right and authority to enter into this Agreement;

(b) It will provide accurate and complete information necessary for Provider to perform Services;

(c) It will comply with all applicable laws and regulations.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 8.1 Limitation

EXCEPT FOR BREACHES OF CONFIDENTIALITY OR INDEMNIFICATION OBLIGATIONS, NEITHER PARTY'S LIABILITY SHALL EXCEED THE TOTAL SERVICE FEES PAID OR PAYABLE UNDER THIS AGREEMENT IN THE [NUMBER] MONTHS PRECEDING THE CLAIM.

### 8.2 Exclusion

NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE LIABLE FOR INDIRECT, INCIDENTAL, CONSEQUENTIAL, SPECIAL, OR PUNITIVE DAMAGES, REGARDLESS OF THE THEORY OF LIABILITY.

### 8.3 Exceptions

The limitations shall not apply to:

(a) Willful misconduct or gross negligence;

(b) Breaches of confidentiality obligations;

(c) Indemnification obligations.

---

## ARTICLE IX: INDEMNIFICATION

### 9.1 Provider Indemnification

Provider shall indemnify Recipient against claims arising from:

(a) Provider's breach of this Agreement;

(b) Provider's gross negligence or willful misconduct;

(c) Provider's violation of applicable laws.

### 9.2 Recipient Indemnification

Recipient shall indemnify Provider against claims arising from:

(a) Recipient's breach of this Agreement;

(b) Recipient's use of Services;

(c) Recipient's violation of applicable laws.

### 9.3 Indemnification Process

The indemnified party shall:

(a) Promptly notify the indemnifying party of any claim;

(b) Cooperate in the defense of the claim;

(c) Allow the indemnifying party to control the defense (subject to the indemnified party's right to participate).

---

## ARTICLE X: INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR

### 10.1 Relationship

Provider is an independent contractor, not an employee, agent, or partner of Recipient. This Agreement does not create a partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship.

### 10.2 No Authority

Provider has no authority to bind Recipient or make commitments on Recipient's behalf, except as expressly authorized in writing.

---

## ARTICLE XI: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 11.1 Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of [STATE], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### 11.2 Dispute Resolution

Disputes shall be resolved through:

(a) Good faith negotiation (30 days);

(b) Mediation (if negotiation fails);

(c) Binding arbitration under [ARBITRATION RULES] (if mediation fails);

(d) Litigation in [JURISDICTION] (if arbitration is not available or fails).

### 11.3 Notices

All notices shall be in writing and delivered to the addresses set forth above (or as updated in writing), by:

(a) Certified mail, return receipt requested;

(b) Overnight courier;

(c) Email (with confirmation of receipt).

### 11.4 Assignment

Neither party may assign this Agreement without the other party's prior written consent, except:

(a) To an affiliate (with notice);

(b) In connection with a merger or sale of substantially all assets (with notice).

### 11.5 Amendment

This Agreement may be amended only by written agreement signed by both parties.

### 11.6 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions shall remain in effect.

### 11.7 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, including all Schedules, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements.

### 11.8 Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
