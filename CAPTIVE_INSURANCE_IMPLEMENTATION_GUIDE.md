## Executive Summary

### What is Captive Insurance?

A captive insurance company is YOUR own insurance company that YOU own. Operating businesses pay premiums to the captive for legitimate business risks. These premiums are:

1. **100% tax-deductible** to operating companies (reduces taxable income)
2. **Tax-free** to the captive (under IRC Section 831(b) election)
3. **Invested tax-deferred** for 10+ years
4. **Distributed at capital gains rates** (15-20%) vs ordinary income (37%+)

**Net Result**: $400K-$1M/year in immediate tax savings + wealth accumulation vehicle

## Tax Compliance & IRS Audit Defense

### Common IRS Challenges

**The IRS scrutinizes captives for**:
1. Lack of risk distribution (not enough unrelated risks)
2. Insufficient risk transfer (premiums not arm's length)
3. Economic family doctrine (all entities owned by same family)
4. Lack of actual claims (premium parking scheme)

### Compliance Checklist

**Required for IRS Audit Defense**:

- [ ] **Independent Actuary**: Annual actuarial study proving premiums are appropriate
- [ ] **Arm's Length Pricing**: Premiums match what third-party would charge
- [ ] **Multiple Operating Companies**: At least 3-5 unrelated businesses (you have 7+) ✅
- [ ] **Actual Claims Paid**: 5-15% of premiums paid out in claims
- [ ] **Board Minutes**: Quarterly board meetings documented
- [ ] **Investment Policy**: Written policy for reserve investments
- [ ] **Claims Manual**: Documented claims procedures
- [ ] **Separate Bank Account**: Captive funds not commingled
- [ ] **Professional Management**: Third-party captive manager (not self-administered)

**Your Structure = STRONG Audit Defense**:
- ✅ Multiple unrelated businesses (notary, tax, consulting, real estate, tech)
- ✅ Legitimate business risks with high claim potential
- ✅ Arm's length premiums (will be actuarially justified)
- ✅ Professional management (will hire Artex or Strategic Risk)
- ✅ Dynasty Trust ownership (legitimate business purpose)

### If Audited

**Timeline**: IRS typically audits 3-5 years after formation

**Defense Strategy**:
1. Provide actuarial reports (prove premium justification)
2. Show claims paid (prove it's real insurance, not tax shelter)
3. Demonstrate independent businesses (overcome economic family doctrine)
4. Professional management proof (not a sham)

**Likely Outcome with Proper Setup**: IRS accepts captive as legitimate

**Penalty if Disallowed**:
- Premiums re-characterized as non-deductible
- Back taxes + penalties + interest
- **Potential cost: $500K-$2M** (reason for proper setup)

**Mitigation**: Work with experienced advisors from day one
