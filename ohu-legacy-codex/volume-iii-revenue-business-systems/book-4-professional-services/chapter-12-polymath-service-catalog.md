## Section 6: The "Least Time" Protocol

To execute this without burning out, you must follow the **Architect vs. Bricklayer** rule.

### 1. Be the Architect
*   **Design** the Tax Plan (High Value).
*   **Design** the Asset Allocation (High Value).
*   **Structure** the Real Estate Deal (High Value).

### 2. Delegate the Bricklaying
*   **Tax Prep**: Junior Accountant or White-Label Firm does data entry/drafting.
*   **Investing**: Software (Altruist) executes trades and rebalancing.
*   **Real Estate**: Junior Agent does showings and inspections.
*   **Admin**: Virtual Assistant (VA) handles scheduling and document chasing.

### 3. The "One Interface" Rule
*   Clients interact with **You** (the Architect) for strategy.
*   Clients interact with **The System** (Portal/App) for logistics.
*   Clients *never* interact with the back-office staff directly (preserves your "Premium" brand).
