# Appendix D: Glossary & Definitions

## Introduction

The language of empire building involves specialized terminology from law, finance, technology, and business. This glossary defines key terms used throughout the Legacy Codex to ensure clarity and common understanding among family members and team members.

---

## D.1 Legal Terms

- **Beneficiary:** A person or entity entitled to receive benefits (assets or income) from a trust, will, or insurance policy.
- **Decanting:** The process of moving assets from an old trust to a new trust with more favorable terms.
- **Directed Trust:** A trust where the administrative duties of the trustee are separated from the investment and distribution decisions (common in South Dakota).
- **Fiduciary:** A person or organization that acts on behalf of another person or persons, putting their clients' interest ahead of their own, with a duty to preserve good faith and trust.
- **Grantor (Settlor):** The creator of a trust who transfers assets into it.
- **Per Stirpes:** A legal term stipulating that if a beneficiary dies before the testator, the beneficiary's share of the inheritance goes to their heirs.
- **Pour-Over Will:** A will that ensures any assets not already in a trust are transferred into the trust upon death.
- **Situs:** The legal jurisdiction where a trust is administered and whose laws govern its validity and construction (e.g., South Dakota).

---

## D.2 Financial Terms

- **Accredited Investor:** An individual with income >$200k ($300k joint) or net worth >$1M (excluding primary residence), eligible to invest in private placements.
- **Asset Allocation:** The strategy of dividing an investment portfolio among different asset categories (stocks, bonds, real estate) to balance risk and reward.
- **Basis points (BPS):** A unit of measure for interest rates and other percentages in finance. One basis point is equal to 1/100th of 1%, or 0.01%.
- **EBITDA:** Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. A proxy for a company's operating cash flow.
- **Liquidity:** The ease with which an asset or security can be converted into ready cash without affecting its market price.
- **Net Worth:** The total value of all assets minus all liabilities.
- **ROI (Return on Investment):** A performance measure used to evaluate the efficiency of an investment.

---

## D.3 Tax Terms

- **Basis (Cost Basis):** The original value of an asset for tax purposes, usually the purchase price, adjusted for stock splits, dividends, and return of capital distributions.
- **Capital Gains Tax:** A tax on the profit realized on the sale of a non-inventory asset.
- **Depreciation:** An income tax deduction that allows a taxpayer to recover the cost or other basis of certain property.
- **Estate Tax:** A tax levied on the net value of the estate of a deceased person before distribution to the heirs.
- **GST Tax (Generation-Skipping Transfer Tax):** A federal tax on transfers to beneficiaries who are more than one generation younger than the donor (e.g., grandchildren).
- **K-1:** A tax form used by partnerships, S-Corps, and trusts to report the beneficiaries' share of income, deductions, and credits.
- **Step-Up in Basis:** The readjustment of the value of an appreciated asset for tax purposes upon inheritance.

---

## D.4 Industry Terms

- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost):** The cost associated with convincing a consumer to buy a product or service, including research, marketing, and advertising costs.
- **Churn Rate:** The percentage of subscribers who discontinue their subscriptions within a given time period.
- **CLV (Customer Lifetime Value):** A prediction of the net profit attributed to the entire future relationship with a customer.
- **KPI (Key Performance Indicator):** A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively a company is achieving key business objectives.
- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue):** Income that a company can reliably anticipate every 30 days.
- **SOP (Standard Operating Procedure):** A set of step-by-step instructions compiled by an organization to help workers carry out complex routine operations.

---

## D.5 Technology Terms

- **API (Application Programming Interface):** A set of functions and procedures allowing the creation of applications that access the features or data of an operating system, application, or other service.
- **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** Technology for managing all your company's relationships and interactions with customers and potential customers.
- **DNS (Domain Name System):** The phonebook of the Internet, translating domain names to IP addresses.
- **SaaS (Software as a Service):** A software licensing and delivery model in which software is licensed on a subscription basis and is centrally hosted.
- **SEO (Search Engine Optimization):** The process of improving the quality and quantity of website traffic to a website or a web page from search engines.
- **Webhook:** A way for an app to provide other applications with real-time information.
