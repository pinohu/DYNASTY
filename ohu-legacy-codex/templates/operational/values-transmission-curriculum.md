## Phase 1: Foundation (Ages 5-10)

### Curriculum Goals

**Primary Focus**: Work Ethic & Basic Money Concepts

**Learning Objectives**:
1. Understand needs vs. wants
2. Learn that money comes from work
3. Practice saving and spending
4. Develop basic work ethic

---

### Curriculum Content

**Unit 1: Needs vs. Wants**
- **Concepts**: 
  - Needs are necessary (food, shelter, clothing)
  - Wants are nice to have (toys, games, treats)
  - We prioritize needs over wants
- **Activities**:
  - Sorting game (needs vs. wants)
  - Shopping exercises
  - Discussion of choices
- **Assessment**: Can child distinguish needs from wants?

---

**Unit 2: Earning Money**
- **Concepts**:
  - Money comes from creating value
  - Work creates value
  - Value creation is rewarded
- **Activities**:
  - Chore system with payment
  - Value creation games
  - Family business exposure
- **Assessment**: Does child understand money comes from work?

---

**Unit 3: Saving vs. Spending**
- **Concepts**:
  - Money can be saved for future goals
  - Money can be spent immediately
  - Saving allows larger purchases
- **Activities**:
  - Three Jars system (Save 50%, Spend 40%, Give 10%)
  - Savings goal setting
  - Tracking savings progress
- **Assessment**: Is child saving money regularly?

---

**Unit 4: Work Ethic**
- **Concepts**:
  - Hard work leads to rewards
  - Effort matters
  - Completing tasks is important
- **Activities**:
  - Age-appropriate chores
  - Task completion tracking
  - Effort recognition
- **Assessment**: Does child complete chores without excessive prompting?
