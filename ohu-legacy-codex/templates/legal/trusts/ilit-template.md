# Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT) Template

## Purpose

This template provides a complete Irrevocable Life Insurance Trust (ILIT) Agreement for holding life insurance policies outside the estate. The ILIT eliminates estate tax on life insurance proceeds and provides creditor protection.

**IMPORTANT**: This template must be reviewed and customized by qualified estate planning counsel before execution. State-specific requirements must be verified.

## Instructions

1. **Identify Parties**: Replace all placeholders with actual names and information
2. **Select Trustee**: Choose independent trustee (cannot be grantor or insured)
3. **Identify Policy**: Complete policy information in Article III
4. **Structure Crummey Powers**: Ensure Crummey withdrawal rights are properly structured
5. **Designate Beneficiaries**: Complete beneficiary designations
6. **Review Tax Implications**: Have tax advisor review for estate tax exclusion
7. **Transfer Policy**: Transfer policy ownership to Trust after execution
8. **Send Crummey Notices**: Send notices annually for premium contributions

## ARTICLE I: TRUST CREATION

### Section 1.1: Trust Purpose

This Trust is created to hold life insurance policies on the life of [INSURED NAME] for the benefit of the Beneficiaries, with the following objectives:

(a) Remove life insurance proceeds from the Grantor's estate for estate tax purposes;

(b) Provide creditor protection for death benefits;

(c) Control the distribution of death benefits to Beneficiaries;

(d) Minimize generation-skipping transfer taxes;

(e) Provide for the health, education, maintenance, and support of Beneficiaries.

### Section 1.2: Irrevocability

This Trust is irrevocable. The Grantor retains no powers that would cause the Trust to be included in the Grantor's estate for estate tax purposes. The Grantor may not:

(a) Revoke or amend this Trust;

(b) Change the Beneficiaries;

(c) Borrow against policy cash values;

(d) Exercise any incidents of ownership over the policies.

### Section 1.3: Governing Law

This Trust shall be governed by the laws of [STATE], without regard to conflict of laws principles.

---

## ARTICLE II: TRUSTEE

### Section 2.1: Initial Trustee

The initial Trustee shall be [TRUSTEE NAME], who shall serve until resignation, removal, or death.

**Trustee Qualifications**: The Trustee must be an independent party who is not the Grantor, the Insured, or a Beneficiary.

### Section 2.2: Successor Trustee

Upon resignation, removal, or death of the Trustee, the successor Trustee shall be [SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE NAME], or if such person is unable or unwilling to serve, [ALTERNATE SUCCESSOR TRUSTEE NAME].

### Section 2.3: Trustee Powers

The Trustee shall have the following powers:

(a) Apply for, own, and maintain life insurance policies on the life of the Insured;

(b) Pay insurance premiums from Trust assets or contributions received;

(c) Borrow against policy cash values if necessary and in the best interests of the Beneficiaries;

(d) Collect death benefits upon the Insured's death;

(e) Invest Trust assets (including death benefits) in accordance with the Investment Policy Statement (if any);

(f) Make distributions to Beneficiaries in accordance with Article IV;

(g) Hire agents, advisors, and professionals as necessary;

(h) Execute all documents necessary to administer the Trust;

(i) Take any other action necessary or appropriate to administer the Trust.

### Section 2.4: Trustee Compensation

The Trustee shall be entitled to reasonable compensation for services rendered, which shall be paid from Trust assets.

### Section 2.5: Trustee Liability

The Trustee shall not be liable for any action taken or omitted in good faith, except for gross negligence or willful misconduct.

---

## ARTICLE III: LIFE INSURANCE POLICIES

### Section 3.1: Policies Held

This Trust shall hold the following life insurance policy(ies):

**Policy 1**:
- Policy Number: [POLICY NUMBER]
- Insurer: [INSURANCE COMPANY NAME]
- Face Amount: $[AMOUNT]
- Insured: [INSURED NAME]
- Owner: [TRUST NAME] (this Trust)
- Beneficiary: [TRUST NAME] (this Trust)
- Policy Type: [WHOLE LIFE/TERM/UNIVERSAL LIFE/IUL]
- Premium Amount: $[AMOUNT] per [MONTH/QUARTER/YEAR]
- Premium Due Date: [DATE]

**Policy 2** (if applicable):
[REPEAT FOR ADDITIONAL POLICIES]

### Section 3.2: Additional Policies

The Trustee may acquire additional life insurance policies on the life of the Insured with the consent of the Trust Protector (if any) or as otherwise permitted by this Agreement.

### Section 3.3: Premium Payments

Premium payments may be made:

(a) From contributions to the Trust by the Grantor or others;

(b) From Trust income or principal;

(c) From policy dividends or cash values;

(d) From loans against policy cash values (if necessary and appropriate).

The Trustee shall use reasonable efforts to maintain all policies in force.

### Section 3.4: Policy Administration

The Trustee shall:

(a) Maintain all insurance policies in force by paying premiums when due;

(b) Monitor policy performance and values;

(c) Review policy values annually;

(d) Coordinate with insurance company on all policy matters;

(e) Notify Beneficiaries of significant policy changes;

(f) Provide annual reports to Beneficiaries regarding policy status.

### Section 4.1: Primary Beneficiaries

- [CHILD 4 NAME] Sub-Trust: [PERCENTAGE]%

**Option C - Direct Distribution**:
[SPECIFY DISTRIBUTION PROVISIONS]

### Section 4.2: Distribution Standards

Distributions from death benefits shall be made in accordance with HEMS standards (Health, Education, Maintenance, and Support) or as otherwise specified in the distribution provisions.

### Section 4.3: Contingent Beneficiaries

If any Primary Beneficiary predeceases the Insured, that Beneficiary's share shall pass to:

[SPECIFY CONTINGENT BENEFICIARY PROVISIONS - typically per stirpes to descendants or to other children]

### Section 4.4: Distribution Timing

The Trustee may:

(a) Distribute death benefits immediately upon receipt;

(b) Hold death benefits in Trust and distribute over time;

(c) Invest death benefits and distribute income and/or principal as appropriate;

(d) Make distributions in a lump sum or installments as the Trustee deems appropriate.

---

## ARTICLE V: CRUMMEY POWERS

### Section 5.1: Crummey Withdrawal Rights

Each Beneficiary who has a present interest in the Trust shall have the right to withdraw contributions made to the Trust within [30] days of receiving notice of the contribution.

**Purpose**: Crummey withdrawal rights allow contributions to qualify for the annual gift tax exclusion ($18,000 per beneficiary per year, adjusted for inflation).

### Section 5.2: Crummey Notice Requirements

The Trustee shall provide written notice to each Beneficiary with withdrawal rights within [7] days of receiving a contribution, specifying:

(a) The amount of the contribution;

(b) The Beneficiary's withdrawal right;

(c) The deadline for exercising the withdrawal right ([30] days from notice date);

(d) The method for exercising the withdrawal right (written notice to Trustee);

(e) The consequences of not exercising the withdrawal right (lapse and addition to Trust principal).

### Section 5.3: Withdrawal Exercise

A Beneficiary may exercise his or her withdrawal right by:

(a) Providing written notice to the Trustee;

(b) Specifying the amount to be withdrawn (up to the Beneficiary's withdrawal right);

(c) Providing instructions for distribution (check, wire transfer, etc.).

### Section 5.4: Lapse of Withdrawal Rights

If a Beneficiary does not exercise his or her withdrawal right within the specified period ([30] days), the withdrawal right shall lapse, and the contribution shall be added to the Trust principal.

### Section 5.5: Lapse Limitations

To avoid gift tax consequences, withdrawal rights that lapse in excess of the greater of $5,000 or 5% of the Trust value may be subject to gift tax. The Trustee shall monitor this and may structure contributions accordingly.

---

## ARTICLE VI: TRUST ADMINISTRATION

### Section 6.1: Accounting

The Trustee shall:

(a) Maintain accurate books and records of all Trust transactions;

(b) Provide annual accountings to the Beneficiaries (or their legal guardians if minors);

(c) File all required tax returns (Form 1041 if the Trust has taxable income);

(d) Maintain records for a minimum of seven years (or as required by law).

### Section 6.2: Tax Returns

The Trustee shall file Form 1041 (U.S. Income Tax Return for Estates and Trusts) if the Trust has taxable income in any year.

### Section 6.3: Tax Elections

The Trustee, in consultation with tax advisors, may make any tax elections available to the Trust, including:

(a) Fiscal year elections;

(b) Distribution elections;

(c) Other elections as may be beneficial to the Trust and Beneficiaries.

### Section 6.4: Policy Administration

The Trustee shall:

(a) Maintain all insurance policies in force;

(b) Monitor policy performance;

(c) Review policy values annually;

(d) Coordinate with insurance company on policy matters;

(e) Notify Beneficiaries of significant policy changes.

---

## ARTICLE VII: TRUST PROTECTOR

### Section 7.1: Trust Protector Powers

The Trust Protector shall be [TRUST PROTECTOR NAME], or its successor, and shall have the power to:

(a) Remove and replace the Trustee (except as limited below);

(b) Modify Trust terms (subject to limitations set forth in Section 7.2);

(c) Change the situs of the Trust (subject to applicable law);

(d) Add or remove Beneficiaries (subject to limitations);

(e) Approve or disapprove distributions in excess of HEMS standard;

(f) Direct investment of Trust assets (if applicable).

### Section 7.2: Limitations

The Trust Protector may not:

(a) Make distributions to himself or herself;

(b) Modify provisions that would cause the Trust to be included in the Grantor's estate;

(c) Exercise powers in a manner that would cause adverse tax consequences;

(d) Remove the Trustee without cause (unless specifically permitted);

(e) Change the Insured or policy ownership provisions.

### Section 7.3: Succession

Upon resignation, removal, or death of the Trust Protector, the successor Trust Protector shall be [SUCCESSOR TRUST PROTECTOR NAME].

---

## ARTICLE VIII: ASSET PROTECTION

### Section 8.1: Spendthrift Provisions

No Beneficiary shall have the power to:

(a) Assign, transfer, or encumber his or her interest in the Trust;

(b) Anticipate distributions;

(c) Subject his or her interest to the claims of creditors, except as provided in Section 8.2.

### Section 8.2: Creditor Protection

The Trust Estate shall be protected from the claims of creditors of the Grantor, Beneficiaries, and Trustees, except:

(a) Claims for child support or alimony (to the extent required by law);

(b) Claims by the United States or any state for taxes (to the extent required by law);

(c) Claims that existed at the time property was transferred to the Trust (subject to applicable statutes of limitations and fraudulent transfer laws).

---

## ARTICLE IX: AMENDMENT AND TERMINATION

### Section 9.1: Amendment

This Trust may be amended by the Trust Protector (if any) or by court order, subject to:

(a) The amendment must not cause the Trust to be included in the Grantor's estate;

(b) The amendment must not adversely affect the rights of Beneficiaries without their consent (if required by law);

(c) The amendment must be in writing and properly executed.

### Section 9.2: Termination

This Trust shall terminate:

(a) Upon distribution of all Trust assets;

(b) Upon court order for good cause shown;

(c) Upon written direction of the Trust Protector (subject to limitations and court approval if required).

---

## ARTICLE X: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 10.1: Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 10.2: Headings

Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

### Section 10.3: Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in multiple counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

### Section 10.4: Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement regarding the Trust and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.
