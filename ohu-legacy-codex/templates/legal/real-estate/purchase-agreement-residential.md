# Residential Real Estate Purchase Agreement Template

## Purpose

This template provides a standard residential purchase agreement for buying real estate. It covers all essential terms including price, contingencies, closing date, and property condition.

**IMPORTANT**: Real estate purchase agreements are highly state-specific. This template must be customized by a real estate attorney familiar with local laws.

## Instructions

1. **Complete Property Details**: Legal description, address, parcel number
2. **Set Purchase Price**: Total price and earnest money deposit
3. **Define Contingencies**: Inspection, financing, appraisal, title
4. **Set Closing Date**: Target closing date and possession date
5. **Specify Terms**: Financing terms if applicable
6. **Review with Attorney**: Must be reviewed by qualified real estate counsel

### 2. Earnest Money Deposit

### 3. Down Payment

[SPECIFY WHO PAYS CLOSING COSTS - typically split between buyer and seller per local custom]

## CONTINGENCIES

### 6. Inspection Contingency

Buyer has [NUMBER] days from execution to conduct inspections. If Buyer is not satisfied with inspection results, Buyer may:
- Terminate this Agreement and receive full refund of earnest money, OR
- Request repairs (Seller may accept, reject, or counter)

### 7. Appraisal Contingency

If property does not appraise for at least the Purchase Price, Buyer may:
- Terminate and receive earnest money refund, OR
- Proceed with purchase at appraised value (if Seller agrees)

### 8. Title Contingency

Seller shall provide marketable title free of liens and encumbrances except:
- [LIST PERMITTED EXCEPTIONS - e.g., easements, restrictions]

### 9. Home Sale Contingency (if applicable)

Buyer's obligation is contingent upon sale of Buyer's current property at [ADDRESS] by [DATE].

---

## CLOSING AND POSSESSION

### 10. Closing Date

The closing shall occur on or before [DATE] (the "Closing Date").

### 11. Possession

Possession of the Property shall be delivered to Buyer:
- [ ] At closing
- [ ] [NUMBER] days after closing
- [ ] [SPECIFIC DATE]

### 12. Prorations

Property taxes, HOA fees, and utilities shall be prorated as of the Closing Date.

---

## PROPERTY CONDITION

### 13. "As-Is" Sale (if applicable)

Property is sold "as-is" with no warranties except as required by law.

### 14. Seller Disclosures

Seller shall provide:
- Property condition disclosure
- Lead-based paint disclosure (if built before 1978)
- HOA documents (if applicable)
- Other disclosures required by law

---

## DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

### 15. Buyer Default

If Buyer defaults, Seller may:
- Retain earnest money as liquidated damages
- Pursue specific performance
- Seek other remedies available at law

### 16. Seller Default

If Seller defaults, Buyer may:
- Seek specific performance
- Recover damages
- Terminate and receive earnest money refund

---

### 17. Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties.

### 18. Governing Law

### 19. Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic signatures.

## Notes

- **State-Specific**: Real estate law varies significantly by state. This template must be customized for your state.

- **Financing**: If using creative financing (subject-to, owner finance), additional provisions are needed.

- **Timeline**: Ensure all deadlines are realistic and achievable.

- **Professional Review**: Always have a real estate attorney review before execution.
