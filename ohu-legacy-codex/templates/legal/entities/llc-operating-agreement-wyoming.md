# Wyoming LLC Operating Agreement Template

## Purpose

This template provides a comprehensive Operating Agreement for Wyoming Limited Liability Companies. This agreement governs the operations, management, ownership, and administration of the LLC.

**IMPORTANT**: This template must be reviewed and customized by qualified business counsel before execution. Wyoming-specific requirements must be verified.

## Instructions

1. **Complete Company Information**: Fill in all company details
2. **Identify Members**: List all members and their ownership percentages
3. **Define Management**: Choose manager-managed or member-managed structure
4. **Set Capital Contributions**: Document initial and future contributions
5. **Establish Distribution Provisions**: Define profit/loss allocation
6. **Customize Terms**: Adjust terms based on specific business needs
7. **Execute**: Have all members sign the agreement
8. **File**: Keep with company records (not filed with state)

## Template

```markdown
# OPERATING AGREEMENT OF [LLC NAME], LLC

## COMPANY INFORMATION

**Company Name**: [LLC NAME], LLC  
**State of Formation**: Wyoming  
**Formation Date**: [DATE]  
**Principal Office**: [ADDRESS]  
**Mailing Address**: [ADDRESS] (if different)  
**Registered Agent**: [REGISTERED AGENT NAME]  
**Registered Agent Address**: [ADDRESS]  
**EIN**: [EIN NUMBER]

---

## ARTICLE I: ORGANIZATION

### Section 1.1: Formation

The Company was formed as a Wyoming limited liability company on [DATE] by filing Articles of Organization with the Wyoming Secretary of State.

### Section 1.2: Name

The name of the Company is [LLC NAME], LLC. The Company may conduct business under this name or any other name approved by the Members in accordance with this Agreement.

### Section 1.3: Purpose

The purpose of the Company is to:

(a) [PRIMARY BUSINESS PURPOSE - e.g., "Provide management, administrative, and business services"];

(b) Engage in any lawful business activity permitted under Wyoming law;

(c) Own, manage, and operate businesses and assets;

(d) Make investments;

(e) Engage in any other activities related or incidental to the foregoing.

### Section 1.4: Term

The Company shall continue in existence until dissolved in accordance with this Agreement or by operation of law.

### Section 1.5: Principal Office

The principal office of the Company shall be located at [ADDRESS], or at such other location as the Members may determine.

---

## ARTICLE II: MEMBERSHIP

### Section 2.1: Initial Members

The initial Members of the Company are:

| Member Name | Membership Interest | Units |
|-------------|-------------------|-------|
| [MEMBER 1 NAME] | [PERCENTAGE]% | [NUMBER] |
| [MEMBER 2 NAME] | [PERCENTAGE]% | [NUMBER] |
| **Total** | **100%** | **[TOTAL UNITS]** |

### Section 2.2: Membership Interests

Membership interests are represented by Units. The total number of Units authorized is [NUMBER], with each Unit representing a [PERCENTAGE]% interest in the Company.

### Section 2.3: Capital Contributions

**Initial Capital Contributions**:

| Member | Contribution Amount | Contribution Type | Units Received |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|----------------|
| [MEMBER 1] | $[AMOUNT] | [CASH/PROPERTY/SERVICES] | [NUMBER] |
| [MEMBER 2] | $[AMOUNT] | [CASH/PROPERTY/SERVICES] | [NUMBER] |
| **Total** | **$[TOTAL]** | | **[TOTAL UNITS]** |

### Section 2.4: Additional Contributions

Additional capital contributions may be made only with the unanimous consent of all Members, unless otherwise provided in this Agreement.

### Section 2.5: Return of Contributions

No Member shall be entitled to the return of any capital contribution except:

(a) Upon dissolution and winding up of the Company;

(b) As otherwise provided in this Agreement;

(c) With the consent of all Members.

### Section 2.6: Transfer Restrictions

No Member may transfer his or her membership interest without the written consent of Members holding a majority of the membership interests, except:

(a) Transfers to family members or trusts for family members (with notice);

(b) Transfers to entities controlled by the Member (with notice);

(c) Transfers required by law or court order;

(d) Transfers in connection with a buy-sell agreement.

### Section 2.7: Admission of New Members

New Members may be admitted only with the consent of Members holding a majority of the membership interests and upon:

(a) Execution of this Agreement or a joinder agreement;

(b) Making required capital contribution;

(c) Payment of any required admission fees.

---

## ARTICLE III: MANAGEMENT

### Section 3.1: Management Structure

The Company shall be managed by [MANAGER-MANAGED/MEMBER-MANAGED].

### Section 3.2: Manager-Managed Provisions

[IF MANAGER-MANAGED]:

(a) The Company shall be managed by one or more Managers;

(b) The initial Manager(s) shall be: [MANAGER NAME(S)];

(c) Managers shall be elected by Members holding a majority of membership interests;

(d) Managers may be removed by Members holding a majority of membership interests, with or without cause;

(e) Managers need not be Members;

(f) Managers shall serve until resignation, removal, or death;

(g) Vacancies shall be filled by Members holding a majority of membership interests.

### Section 3.3: Member-Managed Provisions

[IF MEMBER-MANAGED]:

(a) All Members shall participate in management;

(b) Decisions shall be made by Members holding a majority of membership interests (unless this Agreement requires a different vote);

(c) Day-to-day operations may be delegated to officers or employees;

(d) Each Member has equal voting rights regardless of ownership percentage (unless otherwise provided).

### Section 3.4: Powers of Managers/Members

Managers/Members shall have the power to:

(a) Manage the Company's business and affairs;

(b) Enter into contracts on behalf of the Company;

(c) Hire employees and independent contractors;

(d) Open bank accounts and sign checks;

(e) Borrow money and grant liens (subject to limitations);

(f) Sell, lease, or otherwise dispose of Company assets (subject to limitations);

(g) Make distributions to Members;

(h) Take any other action necessary or appropriate to conduct the Company's business.

### Section 3.5: Limitations on Authority

Notwithstanding Section 3.4, the following actions require the consent of Members holding [PERCENTAGE]% of membership interests:

(a) Amending this Agreement;

(b) Admitting new Members;

(c) Dissolving the Company;

(d) Selling all or substantially all of the Company's assets;

(e) Merging or consolidating the Company;

(f) Incurring debt in excess of $[AMOUNT];

(g) Making capital expenditures in excess of $[AMOUNT];

(h) [OTHER SPECIFIED ACTIONS].

---

## ARTICLE IV: DISTRIBUTIONS

### Section 4.1: Distribution of Profits

Profits shall be distributed to Members in proportion to their membership interests, unless otherwise agreed.

### Section 4.2: Allocation of Losses

Losses shall be allocated to Members in proportion to their membership interests, subject to tax law limitations.

### Section 4.3: Distribution Timing

Distributions may be made:

(a) At such times as determined by Managers/Members;

(b) Upon liquidation of the Company;

(c) As required by law;

(d) [SPECIFY REGULAR DISTRIBUTION SCHEDULE, IF ANY].

### Section 4.4: Tax Distributions

To the extent the Company has taxable income allocated to Members, the Company shall make distributions sufficient to cover Members' tax liabilities on such income, subject to available cash.

### Section 4.5: Withholding

The Company may withhold amounts from distributions as required by law or as otherwise agreed.

---

## ARTICLE V: BOOKS AND RECORDS

### Section 5.1: Books and Records

The Company shall maintain:

(a) Books of account showing all Company transactions;

(b) Membership ledger showing all Members and their interests;

(c) Minutes of meetings (if any);

(d) Copies of all contracts and agreements;

(e) Tax returns and financial statements;

(f) Other records required by law or this Agreement.

### Section 5.2: Accounting Method

The Company shall use the [CASH/ACCRUAL] method of accounting for tax purposes, unless otherwise required by law.

### Section 5.3: Fiscal Year

The Company's fiscal year shall end on [DATE] of each year, unless changed with IRS consent.

### Section 5.4: Member Access

Members shall have the right to inspect Company books and records at reasonable times and for reasonable purposes, upon reasonable notice.

### Section 5.5: Financial Statements

The Company shall provide Members with:

(a) Annual financial statements within [NUMBER] days of fiscal year end;

(b) Tax returns within [NUMBER] days of filing;

(c) Other reports as requested by Members.

---

## ARTICLE VI: MEETINGS

### Section 6.1: Annual Meeting

An annual meeting of Members shall be held each year on [DATE] or such other date as determined by Managers/Members, for the purpose of:

(a) Reviewing Company performance;

(b) Approving financial statements;

(c) Electing Managers (if manager-managed);

(d) Transacting other business.

### Section 6.2: Special Meetings

Special meetings may be called by:

(a) Managers/Members;

(b) Members holding [PERCENTAGE]% of membership interests.

### Section 6.3: Notice

Notice of meetings shall be given at least [NUMBER] days in advance, unless waived by all Members. Notice may be given:

(a) In writing (mail, email, etc.);

(b) Orally (if confirmed in writing);

(c) As otherwise agreed by all Members.

### Section 6.4: Quorum

A quorum for meetings shall be Members holding a majority of membership interests, unless otherwise provided in this Agreement.

### Section 6.5: Action Without Meeting

Any action that may be taken at a meeting may be taken without a meeting if all Members consent in writing (including email).

### Section 6.6: Voting

Each Member shall have voting rights in proportion to his or her membership interest, unless otherwise provided in this Agreement.

---

## ARTICLE VII: TAX MATTERS

### Section 7.1: Tax Classification

The Company shall be classified as a [PARTNERSHIP/DISREGARDED ENTITY] for federal tax purposes, unless an election is made to be treated as a corporation.

### Section 7.2: Tax Elections

The Company may make any tax elections available, including:

(a) S-Corporation election (if eligible);

(b) C-Corporation election;

(c) Other elections as determined by Members.

### Section 7.3: Tax Matters Partner/Member

[IF PARTNERSHIP]: The Tax Matters Partner shall be [MEMBER NAME], who shall represent the Company in tax matters.

### Section 7.4: Tax Returns

The Company shall file all required tax returns and provide copies to Members.

---

## ARTICLE VIII: DISSOLUTION

### Section 8.1: Events of Dissolution

The Company shall dissolve upon:

(a) Unanimous consent of all Members;

(b) Entry of a decree of judicial dissolution;

(c) Any other event requiring dissolution under Wyoming law;

(d) [SPECIFY OTHER DISSOLUTION EVENTS, IF ANY].

### Section 8.2: Winding Up

Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by:

(a) Liquidating Company assets;

(b) Paying Company debts and obligations;

(c) Distributing remaining assets to Members in accordance with their membership interests;

(d) Filing articles of dissolution with Wyoming Secretary of State.

### Section 8.3: Distribution Upon Dissolution

Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed:

(a) First, to creditors (in order of priority);

(b) Second, to Members for return of capital contributions (to the extent not previously returned);

(c) Third, to Members in proportion to their membership interests.

---

## ARTICLE IX: GENERAL PROVISIONS

### Section 9.1: Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by Wyoming law, without regard to conflict of laws principles.

### Section 9.2: Amendments

This Agreement may be amended only with the written consent of Members holding [PERCENTAGE]% of membership interests (or as otherwise specified for particular provisions).

### Section 9.3: Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

### Section 9.4: Entire Agreement

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Members regarding the Company and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

### Section 9.5: Binding Effect

This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Members and their respective heirs, successors, and assigns.

### Section 9.6: Counterparts

This Agreement may be executed in multiple counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

### Section 9.7: Notices

All notices required under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to Members at their addresses set forth in Schedule A (or as updated in writing).
```
