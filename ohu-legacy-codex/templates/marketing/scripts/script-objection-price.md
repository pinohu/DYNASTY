# Price Objection Handling Script

## Purpose

This script provides proven responses to the most common price objections. Use these frameworks to turn price concerns into closed deals.

## Instructions

1. **Listen First**: Let the prospect fully express their concern
2. **Acknowledge**: Show you understand their concern
3. **Reframe**: Shift focus from cost to value
4. **Provide Proof**: Use case studies, testimonials, ROI
5. **Close**: Ask for the business

## Script Framework

### Step 1: Acknowledge

> "I understand that price is an important consideration. Many of our clients had the same concern initially."

### Step 2: Reframe to Value

> "Let me ask you - what's the cost of [NOT SOLVING THEIR PROBLEM]? If [PROBLEM] continues, you're losing [QUANTIFIABLE LOSS] every [TIME PERIOD]."

### Step 3: Provide Proof

> "Here's what [SIMILAR CLIENT] achieved: [SPECIFIC RESULT]. That saved them $[AMOUNT] in the first year alone - a [PERCENTAGE]% ROI."

### Step 4: Address the Concern

**If Budget Constraint**:
> "I understand budget is tight. What if we started with [SMALLER PACKAGE] at $[AMOUNT]? You can always upgrade later once you see results."

**If Comparison Shopping**:
> "I appreciate you're comparing options. The difference is [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR]. That's worth the investment because [BENEFIT]."

**If Need to Get Approval**:
> "I understand you need to get approval. What information would help you make the case? I can provide a ROI calculator and case studies."

### Step 5: Close

> "Based on what you've told me, this solution will [SOLVE PROBLEM] and deliver [BENEFIT]. Would it make sense to move forward?"

## Complete Price Objection Script

**Prospect**: "Your price is too high."

**You**: "I understand that price is an important consideration. Many of our clients had the same concern initially. Let me ask you - what's the cost of not solving [THEIR PROBLEM]? If [PROBLEM] continues, you're losing [QUANTIFIABLE LOSS] every [TIME PERIOD].

Here's what [SIMILAR CLIENT] achieved: [SPECIFIC RESULT]. That saved them $[AMOUNT] in the first year alone - a [PERCENTAGE]% ROI.

I understand budget is always a consideration. What if we started with [SMALLER PACKAGE] at $[AMOUNT]? You can always upgrade later once you see results.

Based on what you've told me, this solution will [SOLVE PROBLEM] and deliver [BENEFIT]. Would it make sense to move forward?"

## Alternative Responses

### Response 1: Break Down the Cost

> "Let me break this down. At $[AMOUNT] per month, that's $[AMOUNT] per day. For that, you get [LIST BENEFITS]. That's less than the cost of [COMPARISON]."

### Response 2: Payment Options

> "I understand the upfront cost is significant. We offer payment plans - you can pay $[AMOUNT] per month over [NUMBER] months. That makes it more manageable, right?"

### Response 3: Risk Reversal

> "I understand you're concerned about the investment. What if I could guarantee [RESULT]? We're so confident, we offer a [GUARANTEE]. If we don't deliver [RESULT], we'll [GUARANTEE TERMS]."

### Response 4: Compare to Status Quo

> "I understand $[AMOUNT] seems like a lot. But what's it costing you to do nothing? If [PROBLEM] continues, you're losing $[AMOUNT] per [TIME PERIOD]. This investment pays for itself in [TIME PERIOD]."

---

## Best Practices

- **Don't Defend**: Don't get defensive about price
- **Don't Discount Immediately**: Try value-based responses first
- **Listen**: Understand the real concern (budget, value, authority)
- **Provide Proof**: Use specific numbers and case studies
- **Create Urgency**: Limited time offers or scarcity
- **Offer Alternatives**: Smaller packages, payment plans, phased approach
